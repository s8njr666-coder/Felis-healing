🐾 Felis-Healing: Bio-Acoustic Frequency & Haptic Therapy
Felis-Healing is a minimalist, high-fidelity Progressive Web App (PWA) designed to simulate the specific mechanical and acoustic properties of a cat's purr for human therapeutic use.
Unlike standard "white noise" apps that use repeating MP3 loops, Felis-Healing uses a Procedural Audio Engine to generate a "living" sound that never repeats, preventing ear fatigue and maximizing neurological benefits.
🧬 The Science
Scientific research indicates that the frequencies between 25 Hz and 140 Hz (the range of a domestic cat's purr) can aid humans in several ways:
Bone & Tissue Repair: Frequencies in the 25–50 Hz range are linked to improved bone density and faster tendon/muscle healing.
Stress Reduction: The rhythmic, low-frequency haptic feedback lowers cortisol levels and blood pressure.
Anxiety Relief: The "Breathing Cycle" (Inhale/Exhale) simulation synchronizes with the user's nervous system to promote deep relaxation.
✨ Key Features
Procedural Synthesis: No audio loops. The sound is generated mathematically in real-time using the Web Audio API.
Haptic Resonance (Android): Synchronized vibration pulses allow you to feel the "laryngeal twitch" of the purr if you place the device on your chest or a sore muscle.
Anti-Robot Logic: Every second of audio contains micro-variations in pitch and volume, mimicking the organic imperfections of a living animal.
ASMR Spatial Audio: Subtle left-to-right panning creates a 3D "sitting in the room with you" effect for headphone users.
TNR / Rescue Mode: A specialized high-frequency preset designed to calm stressed kittens or feral cats during transport.
📲 Installation (Mobile)
This is a Progressive Web App (PWA). You don't need an App Store to install it.
For iPhone (Safari)
Open the live link in Safari.
Tap the Share icon (square with an up arrow).
Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen."
For Android (Chrome)
Open the live link in Chrome.
Tap the Three Dots in the top right.
Tap "Install App" or "Add to Home Screen."
🛠️ Technical Implementation
Web Audio API: Used for sawtooth wave synthesis and Biquad filtering.
LFO Modulation: A Low-Frequency Oscillator creates the 4-second "Breathing Swell."
Vibration API: Triggers haptic transients at ~30Hz intervals.
Service Workers: Enables full offline functionality.
